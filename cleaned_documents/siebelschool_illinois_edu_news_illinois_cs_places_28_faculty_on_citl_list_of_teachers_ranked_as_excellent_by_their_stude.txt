Illinois CS Places 28 Faculty on CITL List of Teachers Ranked as Excellent By Their Students

Illinois CS Places 28 Faculty on CITL List of Teachers Ranked as Excellent By Their Students

3/27/2023
Aaron Seidlitz, Illinois CS

Share on Facebook

Share on Twitter

Share on LinkedIn

Four CS professors were ranked as &ldquo;outstanding&rdquo; on this list &ndash; including Jeff Erickson, Ryan Cunningham, Timothy Chan, and Aishwarya Ganesan &ndash; the highest honor provided by CITL.

Written by Aaron Seidlitz, Illinois CS

This past month, the Center of Innovation in Teaching and Learning (CITL) at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign released their List of Teachers Ranked as Excellent By Their Students for the Fall 2022 semester.

Illinois Computer Science had 28 faculty members included on the list.

&ldquo;Our faculty&rsquo;s commitment to excellence in teaching computer science shines through in many ways, including the number of professors honored by this list from CITL,&rdquo; said Department Head and Abel Bliss Professor in Engineering Nancy M. Amato. &ldquo;This is one of many fitting acknowledgments of our faculty&rsquo;s noteworthy teaching efforts.&rdquo;

Of the 28 total faculty included, the following four ranked as &ldquo;outstanding&rdquo; &ndash; which is the highest honor given through this list by CITL:

Jeff Erickson was ranked as outstanding in CS 473: Algorithms

Ryan Cunningham was ranked as outstanding in CS 498: Law & Policy Issues in CS

Timothy Chan was ranked as outstanding in CS 598: Fine-Grained Algorithms

Aishwarya Ganesan was ranked as outstanding in CS 598: ML for Systems

The other 24 faculty included on the list were:

Ram Alagappan in CS 598: Cloud Storage Systems: Theory & Practice

Lawrence Angrave in CS 341: System Programming

Chandra Chekuri in CS 498: Algorithms for Big Data

Katie Cunningham in CS 500: Current Topics in Computing Education Research

G. Carl Evans in CS 225: Data Structures

Wade Fagen-Ulmschneider in CS 340: Introduction to Computer Systems AND in CS 107: Data Science Discovery

Christopher Fletcher in CS 563: Advanced Computer Security

Yael Gertner in CS 401: Accelerated Fundamentals of Algorithms I AND in CS 491: Excursions in computing

Saugata Ghose in CS 233: Computer Architecture

Jiawei Han in CS 598: Text Mining: A New Paradigm

Geoffrey Herman in CS 233: Computer Architecture AND in CS 500: Current Topics in Computing Education Research

Nan Jiang in CS 542: Statistical Reinforcement Learning

Dakshita Khurana in ECE 407: Cryptography

Robin Kravets in ECE 439: Wireless Networks

Colleen Lewis in CS 491: Intro to Inclusive Terminology

Charith Mendis in CS 598: ML for Compilers & Architect.

Radhika Mitta l in CS 598: High-Speed/Programmable Networks

Klara Nahrstedt in CS 537: Advanced Topics in Internet of Things (IoT)

Luke Olson in CS 598: Scientific Machine Learning

Yongjoo Park in CS 511: Advanced Data Management

Brad Solomon in CS 199: Honors for Data Structures (CS 225)

Elahe Soltanaghai in CS 598: Smart Cities, Homes, & Beyond

Luther Tychonievich in CS 418: Interactive Computer Graphics

Tiffani Williams in CS 400: Accelerated Fundamentals of Computing I AND in CS 491: Excursions in Computing

&ldquo;We teach a demanding subject that constantly evolves,&rdquo; said Mahesh Viswanathan, Illinois CS professor and Associate Head for Academics. &ldquo;Our professors at Illinois Computer Science maintain an impressive command of the subject matter, and relay that to our excellent students in exciting ways.&rdquo;
